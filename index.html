The Lumen Kids standard

We measure what actually matters

Not likes, not comments, not view counts. Our AI analyses the real signals that determine whether a video genuinely teaches — or just keeps kids glued to a screen.

✓
What makes the cut
What our AI scores positively
✓
Slow, deliberate speech Measured in words per minute — not guessed. Calm, clear narration that gives children time to absorb.
✓
Calm, purposeful visuals Simple animations that support the lesson — not compete with it for attention.
✓
Concept repetition and reinforcement Key ideas revisited multiple times so children actually retain what they hear.
✓
Participation prompts "Can you say it with me?" — the hallmark of content designed to teach, not just entertain.
✓
Voice louder than the music Background music should support the narrator — not drown them out.
✕
What gets rejected
Automatic disqualifiers — no exceptions
✕
Talking too fast Rapid speech leaves no processing time. Our AI measures exact words per minute — automatically rejected above threshold.
✕
Flashing or strobing visuals Overstimulating effects that hijack attention and suppress active thinking.
✕
Screaming or high-energy presenters Loud, excitable hosts condition children to need constant stimulation just to stay engaged.
✕
Hyper-fast scene cuts Rapid editing is an engagement trick, not a teaching tool. Our AI detects and rejects it.
✕
Music overpowering the voice When background music dominates, children follow rhythm — not meaning.

🔬

We don't count likes, comments or views.

Most "curated" kids apps use popularity signals — likes, view counts, watch time. We don't. A video with 500 million views can still damage your child's attention span and teach them nothing. We analyse actual content — word by word, frame by frame — using multimodal AI that reads what a child sees and hears. Popularity is not quality. We measure quality.
